Análisis de los mecanismos de resistencia en una cepa de K. pneumoniae

Aquí exploramos diferentes hipótesis para los mecanismos de resistencia observados en una cepa de K. pneumoniae. Las hipótesis se evalúan en función del patrón de resistencia observado.

Antibiograma

  • Test de Carba NP: negativo
  • AMIKACINA: 16 S
  • Ampicilina/sulbactam: >=32 R
  • CEFTAZIDIMA/AVIBACTAM: 0.25 S
  • AZTREONAM: >=64 R
  • BLEE (ESBL): (-)
  • CEFEPIME: >=32 R
  • CEFTAZIDIMA: >=64 R
  • ERTAPENEM: >=8 R
  • IMIPENEM: 2 I
  • MEROPENEM: >=16 R
  • PIPERACILINA/TAZOBACTAM: >=128 R

Interpretación de Hipótesis

  • ESBL mediated resistance: ESBL negative and aztreonam resistance rule out this hypothesis.
  • AmpC mediated resistance: Resistance to cephalosporins, including cefepime, could suggest an AmpC beta-lactamase. However, aztreonam resistance cannot be explained solely by this mechanism.
  • Carbapenemase mediated resistance: The negative Carba NP test does not rule out a carbapenemase, such as OXA-48, especially given the observed carbapenem resistance. However, the resistance to aztreonam does not usually arise from OXA-48 type carbapenemases.
  • Porin loss: Porin loss could contribute to beta-lactam resistance. This could explain the resistance to ertapenem and cephalosporins but wouldn't typically confer aztreonam resistance.
  • Combined mechanism: The resistance profile could be due to a combination of factors, such as a carbapenemase along with ESBLs or AmpC beta-lactamases, and a porin loss. A combination of porin loss with an ESBL or AmpC overproduction can lead to aztreonam resistance.
